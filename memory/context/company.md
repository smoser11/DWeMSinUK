# University of Nottingham

## Context for DWeMSinUK

Both Scott Moser and Caroline Emberson are at the University of Nottingham (UoN), in different faculties of the Faculty of Social Science.

- **Scott:** School of Politics and International Relations, in the Law and Social Sciences (LaSS) building, University Park campus.
- **Caroline:** Nottingham University Business School (NUBS), Operations Management and Information Systems (OMIS) department, in the Si Yuan Building on Jubilee Campus.

Cross-faculty collaboration between Politics & IR and the Business School is somewhat unusual but works here because the research sits at the intersection of (a) qualitative operations-management work on labour exploitation in supply chains (Caroline's home territory) and (b) methodological work on hidden-population estimation (Scott's home territory).

## Other affiliations worth tracking

- **Kalayaan** — UK civil-society organisation campaigning for domestic worker rights since 2008. Cited extensively in the DWeMSinUK paper; one of the partner organisations that helped recruit RDS seed participants.
- **Latin American Women's Rights Service (LAWRS)** — another civil-society partner; published a 2023 report on exploitation in domestic work used in the DWeMSinUK paper.
- **JISC** — UK academic IT services provider. Used to host the online survey instrument.
- **ILO** — International Labour Organization. The Indicators of Forced Labour framework underpins the DWeMSinUK measurement model.
- **NRM (UK Home Office)** — National Referral Mechanism. The official UK count of identified modern slavery victims; used as a baseline.
